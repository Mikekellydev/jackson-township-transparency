Township Trustee and Advisory Board Responsibilities

This page summarizes the legally defined responsibilities of the Township Trustee and the Township Advisory Board as outlined in various sections of the Indiana Code. It is written for residents seeking a clear, accessible understanding of how township government functions.

Township Trustee Responsibilities

Primary Executive Officer
The Trustee is essentially the “CEO” of the township, responsible for executing all township functions.

Key Duties Under Indiana Code

  • Township Assistance (Poor Relief)
    IC 12-20-5.5, IC 12-20-17
    The trustee must administer assistance programs, maintain guidelines, evaluate applications, manage emergency relief, and keep required records.
  • Township Budget Preparation
    IC 36-6-4-11
    The trustee drafts and submits the annual budget to the Advisory Board for approval.
  • Accounting and Financial Reports
    IC 36-6-4-12
    The trustee must maintain all financial records, pay obligations, keep the general ledger, and submit an annual financial report to the State Board of Accounts.
  • Fire Protection Contracts
    IC 36-8-13
    The trustee negotiates and executes fire and emergency service contracts on behalf of township residents.
  • Township Property and Facilities
    IC 36-6-4-3
    The trustee maintains township property, cemeteries, parks, and public buildings.
  • Township Employees
    IC 36-6-4-14
    The trustee may hire, manage, and compensate township employees (subject to appropriations).
  • Trustee’s Designee Requirement
    IC 36-6-4-18
    Within 30 days of taking office, the trustee must designate someone to perform trustee duties if the trustee is absent or incapacitated and notify the Board Chair and County Sheriff.
  • Emergency Management Coordination
    IC 10-14-3
    The trustee cooperates with county emergency management during disasters or major incidents.

Township Advisory Board Responsibilities

Legislative and Fiscal Oversight
The Advisory Board is the township’s governing body that oversees the trustee. Its primary role is financial oversight and checks and balances.

Key Duties Under Indiana Code

  • Budget Review and Approval
    IC 36-6-6-10
    The board reviews, modifies if necessary, and approves the trustee’s proposed annual budget.
  • Appropriations and Additional Funding
    IC 36-6-6-14
    The board approves additional appropriations, transfers, and adjustments when needed.
  • Financial Oversight
    IC 36-6-6-4
    The board examines all claims, expenditures, and financial records presented by the trustee.
  • Township Assistance Oversight
    IC 12-20-5.5-4
    The board adopts the township assistance guidelines proposed by the trustee.
  • Setting Salaries and Compensation
    IC 36-6-6-10(b)
    The board sets compensation for the trustee, employees, and board members themselves.
  • Filling Township Vacancies
    IC 3-13-7
    The board may take part in filling vacancies in the event of trustee resignation, incapacity, or removal.
  • Township Borrowing and Debt
    IC 36-6-6-19
    The board authorizes loans or emergency borrowing when needed.

These responsibilities outline how township government is intended to function under Indiana law, with the trustee acting as the executive manager and the advisory board serving as the fiscal and oversight body.
